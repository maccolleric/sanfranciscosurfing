Organizations Making a Difference

The ocean gives us a lot. These three organizations are working hard to give back — whether that's getting more people in the water, keeping the beach clean, or protecting the coastline for future generations. Find the one that speaks to you and get involved.

Ocean Therapy

MeWater Foundation

MeWater Foundation uses the ocean as a vehicle for healing, wellness, and personal growth. Founded in San Francisco, MeWater runs ocean therapy and surf programs for people experiencing trauma, mental health challenges, homelessness, and other life hardships. The foundation believes in the transformative power of connecting people — especially those who have been disconnected — with the ocean.

Their programs bring participants to Ocean Beach for surf instruction, ocean awareness, and guided ocean experiences that are designed to build confidence, reduce stress, and create community. Many participants have never been to the beach despite living just miles away.

Focus
Ocean therapy, mental health, community wellness
Location
San Francisco — Ocean Beach
How to Help
Surf instructors, beach assistants, program support, donations

Youth Surfing

City Surf Project

City Surf Project is a San Francisco nonprofit dedicated to making surfing accessible to young people from all backgrounds. The organization works primarily with middle and high school students from underserved communities, providing free surf lessons, wetsuit rentals, transportation to the beach, and mentorship from experienced surfers and coaches.

For many of City Surf Project's students, the program is their first time in the ocean. Beyond teaching surfing, CSP uses the experience to build resilience, self-confidence, and a lasting relationship with the natural world. The organization has served thousands of students since its founding in 2012 and partners with San Francisco Unified School District.

Focus
Youth access to surfing, ocean education, mentorship
Location
San Francisco — schools and Ocean Beach
How to Help
Surf instructors, beach coaches, school outreach, fundraising

Ocean Protection

Surfrider Foundation
San Francisco

The San Francisco chapter of the Surfrider Foundation is the local arm of the world's leading nonprofit dedicated to the protection of the world's oceans, waves, and beaches. The SF chapter focuses on issues directly affecting Bay Area coastlines — including water quality, coastal erosion, plastic pollution, and public beach access.

Surfrider SF organizes regular beach cleanups at Ocean Beach and other Bay Area shores, advocates on local policy issues, and runs educational programs about ocean health and environmental stewardship. Their work is driven entirely by volunteers who show up — surfers, swimmers, beachgoers, and anyone who cares about keeping the coast healthy.

Focus
Coastal protection, water quality, beach access, policy advocacy
Location
San Francisco Bay Area coastline
How to Help
Beach cleanups, advocacy, chapter meetings, donations
